About Angelika Belle

Rasta Maria

Afro-Reggaeton R&B Soul Reggae Afrobeats

Born and raised in Port Harcourt, Rivers State, Angelika Belle is a Nigerian singer, songwriter, and performer blending Afro-Reggaeton, R&B, soul, reggae, and contemporary Afrobeats. Her standout trait? A soothing yet powerful voice that fans describe as having a natural autotune effect.

A Theatre Arts graduate from the University of Port Harcourt, Angelika spent six years as lead singer of The Blue Pearls Band, where she sharpened her vocals and stagecraft. After moving to Lagos in 2018 for NYSC, she started posting reggae renditions of Afrobeats songs online, and the internet took notice.

The late Sound Sultan discovered her through her reggae cover of "Faya Faya" and signed her to Naija Ninjas, where she released "Sho'Ma" and "Broken or Not." Now independent, Angelika has worked with Davido, Kizz Daniel, Fiokee, and the Loud Urban Choir. Her hit "Dreadlocks" went viral on TikTok in 2024 with over 15,000 user-generated videos, trending in the US, Haiti, and Nigeria.

"Part Whoopi in Sister Act, part Maria from Sound of Music: strength, spirituality, calmness, and grounded femininity. That's the Rasta Maria energy."

Bio

Angelika Belle is a Nigerian singer, songwriter, and performer whose music fuses Afro-Reggaeton, R&B, soul, reggae, and contemporary Afrobeats. Known for her soothing yet powerful voice, often described as having a "natural autotune" quality, she brings a Gen-Z Bob Marley aesthetic to every stage she commands.

Discovered by the late Sound Sultan through her viral reggae cover of "Faya Faya," Angelika has since collaborated with Davido, Kizz Daniel, and Fiokee. Her 2022 Galaxy Music Award win for Best Alternative Song ("Sho'Ma") and the viral TikTok explosion of "Dreadlocks" in 2024 (15,000+ user-generated videos, trending across the US, Haiti, and Nigeria) have cemented her as one of the most exciting voices in African music.
